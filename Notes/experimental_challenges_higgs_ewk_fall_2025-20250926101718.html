Whats nice about this conference is that because its so focused, you tend to get the people who actually do the analyses So while most conferences are somewaht overrated, I would say Higgs is underrated.

Organizers did a good job putting together an exciting program, and so the plot of my talk is just to follow the program I will say somehting about HIggs perceision measurements, BSM higgs, Di-Higgs and something about the future of the Higgs program

s2 Ok coming first to HIggs precision measuremnts. Coupling measurements is the major part of the program. The Sensitivity is dominated by the "Big 5" ZZ, WW, gg. bb, and, tau tau There is not so much to say here, Experimentally we know what we are doing, these measurements have been done for over 10 yearss, we have achieved O(10%) percisions

If there is an experimental challeges it is in sustaining momentum. These measurements used to be the marques analyses within the collaboration, wth the best people working on them. Now, I get the sense that its hard to attract students and postdocs to them. This is somehing we need to do a better job doing within the collaborations. Saying this here is Like preaching to the choir… This is what this conference series is all about. But we need to remind our collqueses and students and this is really the front lines. In many models this is where we will see the deviation first in bb or ZZ.
